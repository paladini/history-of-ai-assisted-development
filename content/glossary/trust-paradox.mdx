## The paradox

Stack Overflow survey data reveals a striking trend:

| Year | AI Adoption | Trust in AI | Favorability |
|------|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| 2023 | 70% | 40% | 77% |
| 2024 | 76% | — | 72% |
| 2025 | 84% | 29% | 60% |

**Adoption rises while trust and favorability decline.** Developers use AI more than ever but believe it less.

## Why it happens

### Capability outpaces comprehension
Models improve faster than developers can evaluate output quality. What looked impressive in 2023 looks routine in 2025 — expectations reset upward.

### Hallucination awareness
As more developers use AI, more encounter confident-but-wrong outputs. Awareness of limitations grows with experience.

### Security and IP concerns
Enterprise developers worry about code provenance, license contamination, and data leakage. High adoption doesn't mean organizational comfort.

### Vibe coding backlash
The cultural moment of accepting AI output without scrutiny (see vibe coding) prompts pushback from engineering leaders who demand verification.

## Implications

- **Verification workflows** become essential (tests, review, linting)
- **Agent adoption lags** chat adoption — 52% don't use agents (SO 2025)
- **Tool makers** invest in explainability, citations, and diff previews
- **Standards** (MCP, AGENTS.md) aim to make AI behavior more predictable

All statistics sourced from the [Stack Overflow Developer Survey](https://survey.stackoverflow.co/) AI sections for 2023, 2024, and 2025. See our [Data dashboard](/history-of-ai-assisted-development/data/) for interactive charts.
